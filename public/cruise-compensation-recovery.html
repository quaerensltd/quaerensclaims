Common problem

Did your cruise holiday fall short of what was sold?

If your cruise did not match what you were sold, whether because of missed ports, itinerary changes, cabin downgrades, cancelled services or poor onboard conditions, you may have grounds to seek compensation or reimbursement.

Quaerens helps passengers organise the facts, assess the value lost and understand whether a cruise dispute deserves closer review.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • Itinerary changed or key ports were missed
  • • Cabin was downgraded or unsuitable
  • • Advertised facilities or services were unavailable
  • • Hygiene, safety, illness or noise concerns affected the trip
  • • Cruise was shortened, delayed or materially disrupted
  • • The cruise line offered little or no fair resolution

Cruise holiday issues

What can make a cruise case worth reviewing?

  • Itinerary changes or missed ports
    Skipped destinations, shortened stops or substituted ports can reduce the value of the trip.
  • Cabin downgrades
    Being moved to a lower-grade cabin or one with serious noise, view or condition issues can justify review.
  • Onboard facility failures
    Pools, restaurants, entertainment or other advertised facilities being unavailable can reduce holiday value.

Useful evidence can include:

  • ✅ Booking confirmation and cruise contract
  • ✅ Original itinerary and notice of any changes
  • ✅ Cabin confirmation and evidence of downgrade
  • ✅ Photos, videos or onboard complaint records
  • ✅ Receipts for extra costs or lost excursions
