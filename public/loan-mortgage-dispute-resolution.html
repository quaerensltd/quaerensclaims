Common problem

Has your lender handled your account fairly?

Loan and mortgage disputes can arise where balances do not make sense, payments are not applied correctly, interest is calculated incorrectly, or repeated fees appear without a clear explanation.

If the lender has relied on vague responses, delayed a complaint, or failed to correct an obvious issue, a structured review can help clarify whether the handling was fair.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • The wrong interest rate appears to have been applied
  • • Arrears, admin or account fees were added repeatedly
  • • Payments were not credited or allocated correctly
  • • Statements or balances appear inconsistent
  • • Agreed repayment plans or lender promises were not followed
  • • Your complaint was delayed, dismissed or not properly answered
