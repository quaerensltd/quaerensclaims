What Are Loan & Mortgage Disputes?

These disputes arise where a lender applies incorrect rates, adds unfair fees, misallocates payments, or administers the account in a way that causes financial harm or confusion.

  • Incorrect interest rates – Where the wrong rate has been applied or changes were not handled correctly.
  • Unfair arrears or admin charges – Repeated fees that appear excessive, unexplained, or inconsistently applied.
  • Payment misallocation – Payments not credited correctly, leaving balances higher than expected.
  • Statement or balance errors – Account records that do not reflect what should have happened.
  • Failure to apply agreed terms – For example, repayment plans, freezes, or lender promises not being carried out.
  • Complaint handling failures – Where the lender delays, gives vague responses, or does not address the issue properly.

Your Rights With Loans & Mortgages

In the UK and EU, lenders are expected to administer accounts fairly, communicate clearly, and handle complaints properly where disputes arise.

  • Fair treatment – Lenders should apply rates, payments, and charges consistently and in line with the agreement.
  • Clear communication – Statements, fees, and lender decisions should be explained properly.
  • Proper complaint handling – Complaints should be reviewed and answered with a reasoned response.

These protections are supported by consumer credit and mortgage regulation, including complaint frameworks such as the Financial Ombudsman Service in the UK, with similar protections across Europe under national financial and consumer laws.

What Can You Recover?

Where the lender’s handling was wrong or unfair, it may be possible to seek:

  • Correction of overcharged interest – Where the wrong rate was applied or interest was calculated incorrectly.
  • Refund of unfair fees – Including arrears fees, admin charges, or repeated account costs that should not have been applied.
  • Correction of balances and payment allocation – Where the account history or balance does not reflect the payments made.
  • Escalation of lender complaints – Where the lender failed to resolve the matter properly through its complaints process.

A structured review can help determine whether the lender’s administration was handled properly and whether further escalation may be justified.

Do You Qualify for Loan & Mortgage Dispute Resolution?

You may want a formal review if:

  • ✅ You believe you have been overcharged or the account has been mishandled
    This can include rates, fees, payment application, statement errors, or failures to follow agreed arrangements.
  • ✅ You have supporting evidence
    Useful items include statements, agreement documents, rate notices, complaint emails, and lender responses.
  • ✅ The dispute involves meaningful value or complexity
    Especially where the account history is unclear or the lender has not resolved the issue after complaint.
