Situation

Many parking tickets can be challenged

If you’ve received a parking ticket, PCN, or private parking charge, it may be worth checking whether the charge is valid before paying. Many appeals are based on unclear signs, payment problems, incorrect details, unfair enforcement, or genuine mitigating circumstances.

Quaerens helps you turn your situation into a clear, structured appeal letter that you can send to the council or parking operator.

Common appeal grounds:

  • • The signs were unclear, missing, or not visible
  • • The payment machine or app failed
  • • The ticket contains incorrect details
  • • You had a medical emergency or urgent situation
  • • You paid, had a permit, or were allowed to park
  • • The charge is excessive or unfair in the circumstances

What evidence helps?

Keep anything that supports your version of events

  • Photos of signs and road markings
    Useful where signs were unclear, blocked, too small, or not visible from where you parked.
  • Payment proof
    Receipts, bank statements, app screenshots, or payment confirmation emails can be important.
  • Medical or emergency evidence
    Appointment records, breakdown evidence, or emergency details can support mitigation.
  • Ticket and envelope
    Keep the original PCN, reminder letters, envelopes, and screenshots of online portals.
