Situation

Some buyers only discover the real problems after they move in

Many survey concerns only become clear after completion, when damp, roof damage, cracking, timber problems, or hidden deterioration start to show up.

If the warning signs were there before purchase, it may be worth reviewing whether the survey gave a fair and competent picture of the property's condition.

Common issues worth reviewing:

  • • Damp, mould, or water ingress
  • • Roof and exterior defects
  • • Cracks or movement concerns
  • • Hidden defects discovered after purchase
  • • Unexpected repair costs soon after completion

Who may want this review?

You may want to check your survey if:

  • You discovered major issues after moving in
    Especially where the defects became obvious shortly after completion.
  • You relied on the report when deciding to buy
    The survey may have given reassurance that later turned out to be misleading.
  • You now face repair costs you did not expect
    Unexpected works can be a sign the property condition was not fairly presented.
  • The warning signs may have been visible at the time
    A review can help assess whether the report should have highlighted them more clearly.
