Your Rights with a Property Survey

Across the UK, property buyers are entitled to expect that a professional survey is carried out with reasonable care and skill, and that important defects, warning signs, or material concerns are not overlooked or understated.

  • Receive a survey carried out to a reasonable professional standard – A property survey should give a fair and competent overview of the condition of the property based on the type of report you paid for.
  • Expect important issues to be identified or clearly highlighted – Where visible warning signs, deterioration, or concerns were present, these should not be ignored, understated, or left unclear.
  • Challenge unfair or costly outcomes – If serious issues were missed and you later faced unexpected repair costs or property problems, you may have options available.

In the UK, these protections may be supported by consumer rights, professional duties, and legal principles including:

  • Consumer Rights Act 2015 – Helps ensure professional services are carried out with reasonable care and skill.
  • Misrepresentation Act 1967 – If the report gave a misleading or incomplete picture of the property’s condition, this may strengthen your position.
  • Professional standards and duty of care – Surveyors are generally expected to carry out their work competently and in line with the standard reasonably expected of a professional inspection.
