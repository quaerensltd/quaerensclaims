Common problem

Was the property’s condition fairly reported before you bought?

Many survey concerns only become clear after completion, when damp, roof damage, cracking, timber problems, or hidden deterioration start to show up.

If warning signs were present before purchase, it may be worth reviewing whether the survey gave a fair and competent picture of the property’s condition.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • Damp, mould, staining, or water ingress appeared soon after purchase
  • • Roof or exterior deterioration was not clearly reported
  • • Cracks, movement, or structural warning signs were missed
  • • You faced unexpected repair costs after completion
  • • The report gave reassurance that now appears misleading
  • • Photographs or later evidence suggest the issues were visible

Common survey review issues

What can make a survey case worth reviewing?

  • Visible defects may have been missed
    Damp, cracking, roof deterioration, or external warning signs may have required clearer reporting.
  • The seriousness of defects may have been understated
    The report may have mentioned an issue but failed to explain the likely risk, urgency, or cost properly.
  • You relied on the report when buying
    If the survey gave confidence to proceed and major issues later appeared, the report may deserve closer review.

Useful evidence can include:

  • ✅ The survey report or HomeBuyer report
  • ✅ Photos from before and after purchase
  • ✅ Contractor reports or expert findings
  • ✅ Repair quotes, invoices, or estimates
  • ✅ Emails, complaint letters, or surveyor responses
