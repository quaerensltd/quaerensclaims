Common problem

Was your insurer’s decision unfair or unclear?

Travel insurance disputes often arise where medical expenses, cancellation costs, baggage losses or other travel-related claims are rejected on exclusions, technicalities or disputed evidence.

If the insurer’s explanation feels incomplete, unfair or inconsistent with the policy wording, a structured review may help clarify whether the decision can be challenged.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • Medical or emergency expenses were refused
  • • Cancellation or curtailment claim was rejected
  • • Settlement was reduced without a clear explanation
  • • Baggage, valuables or personal items were disputed
  • • Insurer relied on policy wording or exclusions unfairly
  • • Decision has been delayed or not properly explained

Travel insurance disputes

What can make a dispute worth reviewing?

  • Rejected medical or emergency expenses
    Sometimes disputed on treatment necessity, documentation or exclusions.
  • Cancellation or curtailment refusals
    Valid cancellations may be denied on technical or wording grounds.
  • Delayed or underpaid settlements
    Payments reduced or prolonged without a clear and fair explanation.

Useful evidence can include:

  • ✅ Policy schedule and full policy wording
  • ✅ Insurer refusal letter or settlement explanation
  • ✅ Receipts, invoices and proof of payment
  • ✅ Medical notes, booking confirmations or travel documents
  • ✅ Complaint history and insurer correspondence
