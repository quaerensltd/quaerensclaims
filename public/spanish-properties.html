Legal position

Your legal rights in Spain

Bank Liability for Off-Plan Failures

Spanish Law 57/1968 and later court rulings may make banks jointly liable where deposits were accepted into developer accounts without the required guarantees.

Lack of Informed Consent

If mortgage terms, fees, or risks were not explained clearly in your language, that can be highly relevant when assessing the validity of the agreement.

Abusive Clauses

Courts have struck down abusive terms such as floor interest clauses, early repayment penalties, and inflated fees.

EU Consumer Protections

EU law can still matter even where the contract was signed years ago, particularly where unfair terms or omissions are involved.

You may have rights to seek cancellation, refund, compensation, or another form of financial recovery depending on the facts.

Who may be eligible?

You may want to check your position if:

  • You paid for an off-plan property that was never delivered
    Delays, abandonment, or lack of legal completion can be central issues.
  • You never received a bank guarantee
    This can be highly significant where deposits were paid into developer accounts.
  • You were given misleading information
    This includes sales claims, marketing, or mortgage explanations that turned out to be incomplete or inaccurate.
  • You believe the property was unlawful or not properly habitable
    Planning, occupation, or legal-use issues can affect the strength of a claim.
