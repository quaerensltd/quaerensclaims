Common Mis-Selling Practices

  • Promises of high rental income or guaranteed returns – Claims based on expected income that never materialised.
  • Misleading information about site fees or annual increases – Ownership later became unaffordable or unsustainable.
  • Limited or no resale options – Buyers were led to believe resale would be easy, then found themselves locked in.
  • Contracts with unfair terms or hidden clauses – Important conditions were not explained properly before signing.
  • Pressure sales techniques during “free” site visits – Buyers were rushed or manipulated into making a fast decision.
  • Finance agreements that were not properly explained – Credit or finance terms were unclear or affordability was not properly assessed.

If any of the above sound familiar, your case may be worth checking in more detail.

What are your rights?

UK consumer law protects you against unfair, misleading, or aggressive business practices.

  • Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008 – Protects consumers from misleading information and aggressive sales tactics.
  • Consumer Rights Act 2015 – Gives consumers rights to fair treatment, clear terms, and remedies where goods or services are not as described.
  • Misrepresentation Act 1967 – Can apply where false or misleading statements influenced the decision to enter the agreement.

Depending on the facts, you may have grounds to cancel the contract, seek compensation, or both.
