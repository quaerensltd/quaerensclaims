Common problem

Was your Airbnb not what you paid for?

Airbnb stays can go badly wrong where the property is materially different from the listing, unavailable on arrival, unsafe, unhygienic or unsuitable for guests.

Quaerens helps travellers organise the facts, review what went wrong and understand what refund or reimbursement steps may be available.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • Listing photos or descriptions were misleading
  • • The property was unsafe, dirty or unfit to stay in
  • • The host cancelled close to arrival
  • • You were denied access or locked out
  • • You had to pay extra for alternative accommodation or transport
  • • Airbnb refused or only partly resolved your complaint

Common Airbnb issues

What can make an Airbnb case worth reviewing?

  • False or misleading listing
    Important amenities were missing or the property was materially different from the listing.
  • Unsafe or uninhabitable accommodation
    Mould, pests, broken locks, no heating, hygiene issues or lack of basic services may justify a refund.
  • Last-minute cancellation or access problems
    Host cancellation, wrong address details, lockout or an unreachable host can justify a strong complaint.

Useful evidence can include:

  • ✅ Booking confirmation and listing screenshots
  • ✅ Photos or videos of the property issue
  • ✅ Host and Airbnb messages
  • ✅ Receipts for replacement accommodation or transport
  • ✅ Complaint outcome or refund refusal details
