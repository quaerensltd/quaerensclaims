Common Airbnb Claim Situations

  • The listing was false or misleading: Important amenities were missing or the property was materially different from the listing.
  • The accommodation was unsafe or uninhabitable: Problems like mould, pests, broken locks, or lack of basic services may justify a refund.
  • The host cancelled last-minute: You may be entitled to recover booking losses and additional expenses if no suitable alternative was provided.
  • You were denied entry or refused access: Wrong address details, lockout, or an unreachable host can justify a strong complaint.
  • The stay was interrupted or ended early: Safety concerns, double-booking, or serious issues may support a partial or full refund request.
  • Discrimination or abusive treatment: Host behaviour that breaches policy or consumer protections may justify escalation.

What Can You Request Reimbursement For?

  • The full or partial cost of your stay if the property was cancelled, unsafe, or materially different from what was advertised.
  • Transport or alternative accommodation costs where you had to find a last-minute replacement or emergency transport.
  • Distress and inconvenience where the disruption significantly affected your trip or stay.
  • Lost time or missed experiences where the issue caused wider loss during the trip.

What Are Your Rights?

  • Airbnb’s Terms of Service & Guest Refund Policy – Can support refund requests where the host cancels, misrepresents the property, or fails to provide a safe place to stay.
  • EU Consumer Rights Directive – Can help protect consumers where online services are not delivered as promised.
  • UK Consumer Rights Act 2015 – Can apply where accommodation is not as described or not provided with reasonable care.
  • Applicable national consumer laws – Depending on where you booked or where you live, additional protections may apply.
