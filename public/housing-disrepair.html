Common problem

Has your landlord failed to repair your home after you reported the problem?

Housing disrepair occurs when a rented property falls below acceptable living standards because necessary repairs have not been carried out within a reasonable time.

If you have reported the problem and it has not been fixed, it may be worth reviewing whether action can be taken to secure repairs and claim financial redress.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • You reported the issue to your landlord or housing association
  • • Repairs were delayed, ignored or only partly completed
  • • Damp, mould, leaks or poor conditions affected your health
  • • Belongings were damaged by water, mould or unsafe conditions
  • • Rooms became unusable or your daily life was disrupted
  • • You have photos, messages, reports or repair records

Tenant rights

What landlords are usually expected to maintain

Structure and exterior
