Pension advice and transfer issues

Pension problems can affect your retirement for years

Pension losses often become clear long after the advice was given. You may only realise later that a transfer removed valuable protections, increased risk, or left you with a smaller retirement fund than expected.

Whether you worked in multiple countries, transferred a pension into an unsuitable arrangement, or suffered losses after poor advice, it may be worth checking whether compensation or correction is possible.

Common pension concerns include:

  • • Risky or unsuitable pension transfers
  • • High-risk SIPP investments
  • • Missing or underpaid cross-border pension rights
  • • Loss of guaranteed benefits
  • • Poor pension advice or high fees
  • • Unregulated introducer or adviser involvement

Pension issues that may justify a closer look

  • Unsuitable pension transfers
    Advice to leave a safer or guaranteed scheme may deserve review if risks and alternatives were not clearly explained.
  • SIPP and high-risk investment losses
    Some people were moved into investments that were unsuitable for their retirement needs or risk appetite.
  • Cross-border pension gaps
    Where you worked in multiple countries, missing records or poor administration can affect entitlement.
