What Pension Issues Can Be Reviewed?

  • You worked in multiple EU or EEA countries and your pension appears incomplete – Where insurance periods were not properly recognised, pension rights may need to be recalculated.
  • You did not receive updates or proper transfers from abroad – Delays, missed information, or missing pension administration may create financial loss.
  • Your company pension scheme failed or was mismanaged – In some cases, guarantee schemes or insurers may become relevant.
  • You were mis-sold a private pension or advised into an unsuitable transfer – This can include SIPP issues, risky investments, or loss of valuable existing benefits.
  • Your pension was wrongly taxed or double-taxed – Incorrect tax treatment can sometimes be challenged or refunded.

Legal Basis for Your Rights

  • Regulation (EC) No 883/2004 – Helps protect social security and pension rights when people move between EU countries.
  • Directive 2014/50/EU – Supports the preservation of certain pension rights where workers move across borders.
  • National pension protection and guarantee schemes – Local rules may protect certain occupational pension rights where employers fail or schemes collapse.
  • Double taxation treaties – These can be relevant where pension income is being taxed in the wrong way or more than once.
  • Consumer protection and financial services rules – These may apply where pensions were mis-sold or advice was unsuitable.
