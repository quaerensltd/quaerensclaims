ü¶• What is Medical Negligence?

Medical negligence occurs when a healthcare provider fails to meet the standard of care expected, and that failure causes harm or injury to the patient.

  • Misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis
  • Surgical errors (e.g. wrong site surgery, foreign objects left in body)
  • Medication errors or incorrect prescriptions
  • Failure to obtain informed consent before treatment
  • Negligent childbirth care causing injury to mother or baby
  • Ignoring or dismissing symptoms
  • Improper aftercare or discharge
  • Hospital-acquired infections due to poor hygiene

  • A formal apology and explanation
  • Improved care for future patients
