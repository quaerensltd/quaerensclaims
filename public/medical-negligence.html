What is Medical Negligence?

Medical negligence occurs when a healthcare provider fails to meet the standard of care expected, and that failure causes harm or injury to the patient.

It can happen in hospitals, clinics, GP surgeries, or even during routine treatments. If the care you received fell below acceptable standards and you suffered as a result, you may have a valid claim.

These failures can be the result of poor judgment, communication breakdowns, or systemic errors. Here are some common examples:

  • Misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis ‚Äì When a condition is missed or spotted too late, leading to avoidable complications.
  • Surgical errors ‚Äì Mistakes during operations such as operating on the wrong body part or leaving instruments inside the patient.
  • Medication errors or incorrect prescriptions ‚Äì Being given the wrong drug or dosage, causing illness or worsening your condition.
  • Failure to obtain informed consent ‚Äì Not being told about the risks, alternatives, or purpose of a treatment before agreeing to it.
  • Negligent childbirth care ‚Äì Mistakes during labour that harm the baby or mother, such as delays in emergency intervention.
  • Ignoring or dismissing symptoms ‚Äì When medical staff fail to take reported symptoms seriously, leading to delayed treatment.
  • Improper aftercare or discharge ‚Äì Being sent home too early or without proper follow-up, leading to deterioration or readmission.
  • Hospital-acquired infections ‚Äì Contracting infections like MRSA due to poor hygiene or contaminated equipment.
