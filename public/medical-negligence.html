Common problem

Did poor medical care cause avoidable harm?

When you seek medical help, you trust healthcare professionals to act with reasonable care and skill. Medical negligence may occur where care falls below an acceptable standard and causes avoidable injury, worsening health, financial loss or long-term impact.

Claims can involve NHS care, private clinics, hospitals, GP surgeries, dental treatment, mental health care, childbirth, medication or aftercare failures.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • A diagnosis was missed, delayed or incorrect
  • • Surgery or treatment caused avoidable injury
  • • Medication, dosage or prescription errors occurred
  • • Symptoms were ignored or you were discharged too early
  • • You were not properly warned about important risks
  • • You suffered financial loss, pain, care needs or long-term impact

Useful evidence can include:

  • Medical records and letters
    Hospital letters, GP notes, test results, discharge summaries and referral records can help build the timeline.
  • Complaint history
    Emails, letters, phone notes and formal complaints can show what concerns were raised and when.
  • Symptoms and impact
    Photos, diary notes, prescriptions, rehabilitation records and care needs may help show the effect.
  • Financial losses
    Receipts, payslips, travel costs, private treatment invoices and care costs can all be relevant.

Important timing

Don’t wait — time limits may apply

In many medical negligence cases, strict time limits can apply. The relevant date may depend on when the incident happened, when you became aware of harm, and whether the case involves a child or a person lacking mental capacity. It is usually better to review the position as early as possible.
