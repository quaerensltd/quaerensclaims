Common Reasons a Contract May Be Invalid

  • Cooling-off period violations – Advance payment during the withdrawal period can be a serious issue.
  • Missing legal disclosures – Required information may not have been explained clearly or properly documented.
  • Perpetuity clauses – Contracts that tie owners in indefinitely or pass obligations to heirs have been widely challenged.
  • Sales misrepresentation – False resale promises, investment claims, or misleading exclusivity statements may all be relevant.

The European Legal Position – Explained Simply

  • You may have a right to cancel without penalty during the withdrawal period.
  • Advance payments during that period are generally prohibited.
  • Sellers should provide detailed written information in the correct form and language.
  • If those requirements were not met, the contract may be challengeable and funds paid may need to be reviewed.

Notable Spanish Court Verdicts in Favour of Consumers

  • Spanish Supreme Court (2015): Contracts signed after January 5, 1999, must not exceed 50 years, making many perpetual agreements challengeable.
  • La Pinta Beach Resort case (2018): A British couple reportedly recovered a substantial award where the contract included unlawful features and lacked transparency.
  • Marriott Marbella Beach Resort case (2024): A further major consumer ruling reportedly turned on missing detail about the specific property and use period.
