Mis-Sold Spanish Mortgages

Many foreigners were persuaded by estate agents, banks, or brokers to take out Spanish mortgages that were inappropriate, complex, or even unlawful. These were often pushed as part of "investment packages" and signed without the buyer understanding the long-term financial consequences.

  • ✅ Most mortgage agreements signed before 2019 qualify for legal challenge.
  • ✅ Even if your mortgage has been fully repaid, you may still have a valid claim.

What Went Wrong?

  • Foreign Currency Loans– Banks offered mortgages in Swiss francs, Japanese yen, or other currencies to non-Spanish buyers, claiming these loans had “lower interest.” In reality, these were high-risk and unstable products.
  • Lack of Transparency– Many agreements were only in Spanish, lacked proper breakdowns, and failed affordability checks — violating Spanish and EU laws (Directive 2014/17/EU).
  • Unregulated “Equity Release” Schemes – Sold to retirees as pension boosters, these were often unregulated and highly risky.

Your Legal Rights in Spain

    Spanish law and Supreme Court rulings now support claims based on mis-selling, lack of transparency, and missing bank guarantees.

    • Bank Liability for Off-Plan FailuresSpanish Law 57/1968 and later Supreme Court rulings make banks jointly liable if they accepted deposits into developer accounts without issuing the required guarantees.
    • Lack of Informed Consent Equals Invalid ContractIf your mortgage terms (interest rate, fees, risks) were not explained clearly in your language, the agreement may be declared null due to lack of informed consent.
    • Abusive Clauses Can Be Removed or RefundedCourts have struck down abusive terms — such as floor interest rates, early repayment penalties, and inflated valuation fees — with refunds granted.
    • EU Directive 93/13 on Unfair Terms AppliesEven if your contract was signed years ago, EU consumer protection law allows you to challenge unfair terms or omissions — retroactively.

    You have the right to cancel your mortgage, get refunded, or be compensated by the bank.
