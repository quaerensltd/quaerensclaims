Common problem

Were the savings, performance or finance explained properly?

Solar panels are often sold using claims about reduced energy bills, income, grants, finance affordability or long-term savings. If those claims were exaggerated or unclear, you may have been misled.

Problems can also arise where the system was badly installed, underperforms, becomes unsafe, or the installer fails to honour warranties and repairs.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • You were told the panels would “pay for themselves”
  • • Finance costs, interest or affordability were unclear
  • • Energy savings were exaggerated
  • • The system does not perform as promised
  • • Installation defects or electrical issues appeared
  • • Warranty or repair requests were ignored

Your rights

What should you expect from a solar provider?

A solar provider should present the system, cost, finance and likely performance clearly and fairly. Installation should be carried out with reasonable care and skill.

  • Clear and fair sales information — savings, payback periods and finance costs should not be exaggerated or hidden.
  • A properly functioning system — panels, inverters, batteries and related components should operate safely and as described.
  • Reasonable repairs and warranty support — providers should respond appropriately to faults, service issues and warranty concerns.
