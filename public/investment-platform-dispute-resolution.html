What Are Investment Platform Disputes?

These disputes arise when a broker or platform restricts access, delays withdrawals, mishandles orders, or applies its rules inconsistently, leading to financial loss or unfair outcomes.

  • Blocked or delayed withdrawals – Funds held due to unexplained reviews or restrictions.
  • Account freezes or sudden limitations – Trading halted or features removed without a clear explanation.
  • Execution or order disputes – Slippage, delays, rejected orders, or cancelled trades.
  • Platform outages and access failures – Inability to trade during key periods or market volatility.
  • Pricing or incorrect fill disputes – Disputed prices, spreads, or order outcomes.
  • Margin and liquidation disputes – Forced actions that appear unfair or incorrect.

Useful evidence

What can help support a platform dispute?

  • Account statements and transaction history
    These can help confirm balances, holdings, withdrawals, deposits and account actions.
  • Order logs and timestamps
    Trade times, quoted prices, execution prices and rejection messages can be important.
  • Platform messages and complaints
    Emails, chat logs, complaint responses and support tickets can show how the provider handled the issue.

Useful evidence can include:

  • ✅ Account statements and balance records
  • ✅ Withdrawal confirmations or rejected transfer notices
  • ✅ Trade history, order logs and timestamps
  • ✅ Screenshots of errors, outages or restrictions
  • ✅ Platform emails, chats and complaint correspondence

Your rights

What should investment platforms and brokers do?

In the UK and EU, customers have protections where regulated brokers or platforms mishandle accounts, apply restrictions unfairly, or fail to deal with complaints properly.

  • Fair treatment and consistent application of terms — rules and restrictions should be applied fairly and explained properly.
  • Proper complaint handling — firms should investigate complaints and provide a reasoned response.
  • Appropriate remedies where errors occur — where mistakes or failures caused loss, correction or redress may be possible.

What Can You Recover?

Where a platform’s handling was unfair or incorrect, it may be possible to seek:

  • Release of blocked funds – Where withdrawals remain delayed or held without reasonable justification.
  • Correction of account restrictions – Where access was limited or removed in a way that appears inconsistent or unfair.
  • Trade execution or pricing corrections – For rejected orders, cancelled trades, disputed fills, or pricing issues.
  • Escalation of platform failure complaints – Where outages or access failures contributed to losses or missed opportunities.

A structured review can help determine whether the provider’s actions were handled properly and whether further escalation may be justified.

Do You Qualify for Investment Platform Dispute Resolution?

You may want a formal review if:

  • ✅ You experienced restrictions, blocked withdrawals, or disputed trading outcomes
    This can include freezes, KYC or AML holds, execution issues, pricing disputes, liquidation concerns, or outage-related problems.
  • ✅ You have supporting evidence
    Useful items include account statements, screenshots, order history, timestamps, emails, chats, and complaint correspondence.
  • ✅ The dispute involves meaningful value or complexity
    Especially where the amount at stake, urgency, or platform resistance makes specialist review worthwhile.
