Lost Luggage

If your baggage never arrived or was later declared lost, you may be able to claim compensation for the value of the missing items and the disruption caused.

Delayed Baggage

If your luggage arrived late, you may be entitled to recover reasonable expenses for essentials like toiletries, clothing, and other necessary purchases.

Damaged Baggage

If your suitcase or belongings were damaged during transit, you may have grounds to seek repair costs, reimbursement, or compensation.

Your Rights for Lost, Delayed or Damaged Luggage

  • Lost bags: Up to around €1,600 / £1,350 may be claimable under the Montreal Convention.
  • Delayed bags: You may be able to recover reasonable expenses for essential items bought while waiting.
  • Damaged bags: Airlines may have to repair or compensate for baggage damage.
  • These protections can apply across major airlines and international flights.
  • Claims may be relevant for Ryanair, easyJet, BA, Lufthansa, Wizz Air, KLM, Vueling, Jet2, and more.

All you usually need is your flight and bag details, plus whatever proof of loss, delay, or damage you have available.

What evidence helps?

  • Boarding pass or booking confirmation
  • Baggage tag from check-in
  • Property Irregularity Report (PIR) from the airport
  • Receipts for essential purchases
  • Photos of damage
  • Email or chat evidence from the airline

You do not need everything. Start with whatever documents or evidence you already have.
