What Are Gym Membership Cancellation Rights?

If your gym refuses to let you cancel, keeps charging after notice, or won’t refund prepaid months, you are protected by consumer law. The Consumer Rights Act 2015 and EU Directives require contracts to be fair and transparent—meaning you can usually cancel if:

  • You’ve moved house, lost your job, or can’t use the gym due to illness/injury
  • The gym changed its opening hours, facilities, or terms after you joined
  • You gave valid notice but are still being charged
  • Unfair terms or long minimum periods—these are often unenforceable

Your rights apply whether you joined in person, online, or by phone. Quaerens makes it fast and free to generate the correct paperwork—so you can request a refund or cancellation with no fees, no commission, and no legal jargon.

Your Rights When Cancelling or Claiming a Gym Refund

UK and EU law gives you strong protections against unfair contract terms or charges. If you can’t use your membership due to illness, redundancy, or unfair treatment, you can usually cancel without penalty—and may get a refund for prepaid time.

  • Notice Periods: Gyms must not enforce excessive notice or minimum terms if your circumstances have changed.
  • Refunds: If you prepaid and cannot use your membership, you can often claim back the unused portion.
  • Upfront Fees: Joining fees may be refundable if you never used the gym or cancelled within cooling-off.

Tip: Always give notice in writing and keep all your payment records.
