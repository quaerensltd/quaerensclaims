Common problem

Did a dealer or broker earn commission from your finance deal?

If you took out Personal Contract Purchase (PCP) or Hire Purchase (HP), it may be worth checking whether commission, interest or key costs were properly explained before you signed.

Quaerens helps drivers review finance agreements where important information may not have been made clear or where the deal may have been arranged unfairly.

You may have grounds for a review if:

  • • You were not told the dealer or broker could receive commission
  • • The interest rate or total cost was not clearly explained
  • • You were not shown clear pre-contract information
  • • The finance was unsuitable or unaffordable
  • • You felt rushed into signing the agreement
  • • You do not understand how the finance was arranged

What is car finance mis-selling?

  • The dealer or broker received commission from the lender and this was not clearly explained.
  • The size or structure of commission may have affected the terms you were offered.
  • The finance deal, total cost, interest rate or balloon payment was not explained clearly.
  • You were offered PCP or HP finance that may have been unsuitable or unaffordable.

In many cases, drivers were not given clear enough information to understand how the finance was being arranged or how much it was really costing them.

Useful evidence

What documents can help?

  • Finance agreement
    PCP, HP or loan paperwork showing the lender, agreement date, amount borrowed and term.
  • Pre-contract documents
    Key facts, explanations, dealer paperwork, quotes, emails or sales messages.
  • Payment history
    Statements, settlement letters or records showing what you paid and when.
