What Is Car Finance Mis-Selling?

  • You were not told the dealer or broker was receiving commission from the lender.
  • The size of the commission may have influenced the terms you were offered.
  • The finance deal was not clearly explained or the total cost was misrepresented.
  • You were offered a PCP or HP agreement that was unsuitable or unaffordable without proper checks.

In many cases, consumers were not given clear enough information to understand how the finance was being arranged or how much it was really costing them.

The Legal Framework Behind Your Claim

  • Consumer Credit Directive – Requires lenders to provide clear pre-contract information, including costs and the nature of the agreement.
  • Unfair Commercial Practices Directive – Prohibits misleading actions or omissions, including hidden commission arrangements or misleading affordability claims.
  • National financial conduct rules – Additional rules may apply depending on where the agreement was sold and who arranged it.

Did You Know About the Commission?

  • Most customers were never told about these commissions. Many consumers paid more without realising part of the cost was driven by hidden commission structures.
  • This lack of transparency is being examined closely. Regulators and courts have been looking at whether these practices were unfair to consumers.
  • More consumers are now checking historic agreements. Many drivers are only now realising their deal may have cost more than it should have.

What You Could Claim

If your agreement included hidden or excessive charges, you may be entitled to recover part of the interest or other finance-related costs.
If you were misled into a deal that was not in your best interests, there may be a basis to seek compensation for the financial disadvantage caused.
In some cases, where a mis-sold agreement led to problems with payments or adverse records, credit-related consequences may also need to be reviewed.
Where serious non-disclosure or unfairness is involved, the outcome may go beyond a simple interest refund and require a broader review of the agreement.
