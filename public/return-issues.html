Your rights

Your rights under UK and EU consumer law

14-day online cooling-off period

For many online purchases, you may have the right to cancel and return goods within 14 days without giving a reason.

Faulty or misdescribed goods

If the product is defective, damaged, incomplete, or not as described, you may be entitled to a refund, repair, or replacement.

Fair and transparent return terms

Sellers should make return terms clear and cannot rely on hidden, unreasonable, or misleading conditions.

Prompt refunds

Refunds should be processed within a reasonable timeframe, often within 14 days once the seller receives returned goods.

Sellers who fail to comply with consumer law may be liable for the refund, reasonable related costs, and in some cases further compensation.

Who can make a claim?

You may have a return issue claim if:

  • You returned something and never received your refund
    If you sent the item back but the seller failed to issue a refund, it is worth checking.
  • The seller refused the return without legal justification
    Sellers usually need a fair and lawful reason to reject a valid return.
  • You were charged an excessive return or restocking fee
    Fees should be reasonable and clearly disclosed before purchase.
  • The item was faulty, late, damaged, or not as described
    These issues often trigger stronger consumer rights.
  • You were pressured to waive your return rights
    Traders should not mislead consumers into giving up legal protections.
