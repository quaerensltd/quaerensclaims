What Is Mortgage Review?

Mortgage Review is designed to help identify whether your mortgage, remortgage, or secured loan may have been arranged unfairly, unaffordably, or with excessive fees — often due to poor advice, lack of affordability checks, or unsuitable recommendations at the time.

  • ✅ Unaffordable lending – If your mortgage or secured loan was approved despite obvious financial strain, arrears, or unsustainable repayments, it may not have been arranged properly.
  • ✅ Unsuitable mortgage advice – If you were guided into the wrong mortgage, remortgage, or secured loan product for your circumstances, this may raise serious concerns.
  • ✅ Excessive broker or arrangement fees – Many borrowers paid substantial fees without being properly informed of alternatives, costs, or whether the recommendation was truly suitable.

  • ✅ High-cost secured loans – In some cases, borrowers were placed into expensive second-charge or homeowner loans when better options may have existed.
  • ✅ Lack of clear explanation – If important risks, costs, or product limitations were not clearly explained before you signed, your arrangement may need reviewing.

Your Rights with Mortgages, Remortgages & Secured Loans

Across the UK, consumers are entitled to fair treatment when financial products are arranged, recommended, or sold. If your mortgage, remortgage, or secured loan was handled unfairly, you may have options available.

  • Receive clear and fair information – You should have been given clear information about fees, affordability, risks, repayment structure, and the nature of the product before agreeing.
  • Expect suitable recommendations – If advice was given, it should have been appropriate for your circumstances, needs, and long-term financial position.
  • Challenge unfair outcomes – If you suffered financial loss, unnecessary costs, or were placed into an unsuitable arrangement, you may be able to seek a remedy.

In the UK, these protections may be supported by rules and legal principles including:

  • Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) principles and mortgage rules – Firms must treat customers fairly and, where advice is given, ensure recommendations are suitable and responsibly made.
  • Consumer Credit Act 1974 – This may apply in certain secured loan and second-charge lending situations, particularly where the agreement or conduct was unfair.
  • Misrepresentation Act 1967 – If key facts, risks, or costs were not properly disclosed or were presented misleadingly, this may strengthen your position.
