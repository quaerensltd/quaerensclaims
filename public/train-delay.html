Situation

Rail disruption can cost you time and money

If your train was delayed or cancelled and you arrived late at your destination, you may be entitled to compensation depending on the operator, the delay length, and the type of journey.

Claims may also arise where disruption caused missed connections, stranded travel, overnight stays, taxis, or replacement tickets that you had to pay for yourself.

Common claim triggers include:

  • • Delays of 30 minutes or more
  • • Cancelled trains with no suitable alternative
  • • Missed onward connections
  • • Unexpected hotel, taxi, or ticket costs
  • • Significant disruption to the planned journey

What counts as a train delay claim?

When it may be worth claiming

  • Delayed by 30 minutes or more
    Many operators offer partial or full refunds from 30 minutes onwards.
  • Cancelled without a suitable alternative
    If your service was cancelled and no reasonable replacement was offered, you may have grounds to claim.
  • Missed a connection
    If the disruption caused missed onward travel, the wider journey may need to be considered.
  • Out-of-pocket costs
    Extra transport, hotel, or meal costs may sometimes be recoverable.
