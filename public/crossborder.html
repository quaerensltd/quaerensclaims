Cross-Border Creditor Services and Enforcement of Judgments

At Quaerens, we provide professional support to clients who have successfully obtained court judgments or awards, yet encounter significant obstacles when attempting to enforce their claims against corporate defendants. In particular, we assist clients facing complex international structures, where the debtor entity claims to be without assets or financial means, while the controlling parent company — often located in another jurisdiction — has entered into insolvency proceedings.

Quaerens is equipped to navigate these cross-border challenges with the objective of protecting and advancing creditor rights across multiple jurisdictions.

Typical Challenges Faced by Clients

In an increasingly globalized business environment, it is not uncommon for companies to:

  • Shield assets behind a network of subsidiaries and affiliates;
  • Declare insolvency or allege lack of funds in one jurisdiction;
  • Continue operating and incurring obligations elsewhere under the oversight of a parent company;
  • Initiate voluntary insolvency proceedings in the parent company's country of incorporation, often limiting the ability of foreign creditors to recover their claims.

Such situations often leave judgment creditors in a vulnerable position, requiring specialized cross-border action to assert and preserve their rights.

How Quaerens Assists Clients

  1. Identification and Legal Analysis
    • Tracing and identifying the corporate structure, including the parent company and any affiliated entities.
    • Conducting a thorough analysis of the insolvency proceedings initiated by the parent company, including review of applicable insolvency laws and creditor rights in the relevant jurisdiction.
  2. Creditor Registration and Representation
    • Preparing and submitting creditor claims within the insolvency proceedings of the parent company.
    • Ensuring timely compliance with all procedural requirements and deadlines applicable under the insolvency regime.
    • Representing the client's interests in creditors' meetings, voting on insolvency plans where applicable, and communicating with insolvency administrators or liquidators.
  3. Enforcement and Asset Recovery Strategies
    • Evaluating the feasibility of recognition and enforcement of the original judgment in the jurisdiction of the parent company's insolvency.
    • Collaborating with local experts to identify assets that may be subject to enforcement.
    • Taking strategic steps where appropriate to contest fraudulent conveyances, preferential transactions, or other actions detrimental to creditor interests.

Importance of Acting Promptly

It is essential for creditors to act without undue delay once insolvency proceedings are announced or suspected. Many jurisdictions impose strict deadlines for the submission of creditor claims, and late filings may result in the loss of rights to participate in distributions or assert claims effectively.

Moreover, early creditor participation often strengthens the ability to influence the direction of the proceedings and to safeguard against unfair or preferential treatment of certain creditors.
