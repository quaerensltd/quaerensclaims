What Is a Data Breach?

    A data breach happens when personal information — such as your name, email, address, bank details, passwords, or ID numbers — is:

    • Accessed without your permission
    • Stolen by hackers or leaked due to poor security
    • Shared with third parties unlawfully
    • Used for fraud, phishing, or identity theft

    Under European and UK data protection laws, companies are legally required to safeguard your data. If they fail, you may have a right to compensation — even if you haven’t suffered financial loss yet.

What Can You Claim Compensation For?

  • ✅ Emotional distress: Anxiety, stress, or loss of trust caused by your personal information being exposed
  • ✅ Financial damage: Fraud, stolen funds, or time spent securing accounts
  • ✅ Breach of privacy rights: Unlawful sharing or profiling without consent
  • ✅ Failure to notify: If you weren’t informed promptly (within 72 hours) of a breach

Your Rights Under GDPR and UK Law

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)
  • Data Protection Act 2018 (UK)
  • ePrivacy Directive (for online tracking/cookies)

Organisations must:

  • Use appropriate security measures
  • Notify you and regulators of data breaches
  • Allow you to access and control your data
  • Compensate you if their actions caused harm
