What counts?

What is a data breach?

Unauthorised Access

Your information was accessed by someone who should not have had permission to view or use it.

Poor Security

Data was stolen, leaked, or exposed because systems, passwords, processes, or safeguards were inadequate.

Unlawful Sharing

Personal data was shared with third parties without a lawful reason, clear explanation, or valid consent.

Fraud or Identity Misuse

Breached data was used for scams, phishing, account takeover, impersonation, or identity theft.

What can be claimed?

Compensation may cover more than direct money loss

  • Emotional distress
    Anxiety, stress, loss of trust, or feeling unsafe after your personal information was exposed.
  • Financial damage
    Fraud, stolen funds, account recovery costs, credit monitoring, or time spent securing accounts.
  • Privacy rights breach
    Unlawful sharing, profiling, or misuse of personal data without a proper lawful basis.
  • Failure to notify
    Where an organisation failed to tell affected people promptly, increasing the impact of the breach.

Your rights

GDPR and UK data protection rights

EU GDPR

Gives individuals control over personal data and places strict duties on organisations handling it.

UK GDPR

Provides similar protections for individuals in the UK after Brexit.

Data Protection Act 2018

UK legislation that supports and supplements data protection rights and responsibilities.

ePrivacy Rules

Relevant where cookies, trackers, electronic marketing, or online profiling are involved.
