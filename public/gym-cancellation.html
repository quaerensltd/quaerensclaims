Cancel Your Gym—Or Get A Refund!

If your gym is refusing to cancel your membership, or you want a refund for time you couldn't use, you have strong legal rights in the UK and EU. Consumer law protects you against unfair contract terms, excessive notice periods, and “rolling” contracts you no longer want.

  • Cancellation: End your contract at any time for valid reasons (moving, illness, cost-of-living, closure, or unfair terms).
  • Refunds: If you prepaid for months you can't use, you may be entitled to a refund for unused time.
  • Cooling-Off: You can always cancel within 14 days of signup (for online/phone sales or some gym contracts).
  • Unfair Terms: Consumer Rights Act 2015 and EU Directives protect you from unfair or one-sided contracts.

What Are My Rights?

  • Consumer Rights Act 2015 (UK): Contract terms must be fair and transparent. Unreasonable cancellation or notice periods may be unenforceable.
  • EU Consumer Rights Directive: Gives extra protection, especially for contracts sold online/phone—14-day cancellation right applies.
  • Financial hardship, health, or relocation can justify early termination with no penalty in many cases.
  • Unfair Terms: If the gym contract is one-sided or the gym changes terms after you join, you may be able to cancel/refund immediately.
