Situation

Crypto scams can look convincing until withdrawals stop

Many victims are shown realistic dashboards, fake profits, persuasive account managers, and professional-looking websites. The problem often becomes clear only when withdrawals are delayed, blocked, or made conditional on extra payments.

A structured review can help organise the evidence, understand the payment route, and identify whether any complaint, chargeback, tracing, or legal recovery path may be realistic.

Crypto scam warning signs:

  • • Guaranteed or unusually high returns
  • • Pressure to deposit more money
  • • Withdrawal “taxes” or release fees
  • • Fake trading accounts or dashboards
  • • Contact through social media or dating apps

Common scam types

Crypto fraud can appear in many forms

Fake Investment Schemes

Platforms promise high returns but prevent withdrawals once funds have been deposited.

Phishing & Wallet Scams

Fake login pages, support agents, or wallet links are used to steal credentials and empty accounts.

Social Media Scams

Fraudsters use fake profiles, hacked accounts, celebrity-style promotions, and fake giveaways.

Romance or Impersonation Fraud

Scammers build trust over time, then persuade victims to invest or transfer crypto.

Ponzi or MLM Crypto Schemes

Victims are encouraged to recruit others or invest based on unrealistic returns.

Rug Pulls & Fake Tokens

A token is promoted, liquidity is withdrawn, and investors are left with worthless holdings.

⚠️ Important note on expectations

Crypto recovery is not always possible, and anyone guaranteeing recovery should be treated with caution. Recovery prospects are usually stronger where a bank, card provider, regulated exchange, identifiable party, or clear evidence trail is involved.

What can be reviewed?

Potential recovery routes depend on how the money moved

  • Blockchain tracing
    Wallet addresses and transaction hashes may help identify exchanges or routes used after the transfer.
  • Bank or card complaints
    If money was sent by card or bank transfer, there may be complaint or reimbursement routes depending on the facts.
  • Exchange complaints
    Where a regulated platform was involved, evidence may support an escalation or information request.
  • Legal or regulatory action
    Some cases may justify reporting, civil action, or formal complaints where parties can be identified.
