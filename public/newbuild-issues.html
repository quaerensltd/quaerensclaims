What Are New Build Issues?

New Build Issues arise when a newly built property is handed over with defects, unfinished works, poor workmanship, or problems that should have been addressed by the developer, builder, or warranty provider.

  • ✅ Snagging problems – Cosmetic and finishing issues such as poor paintwork, damaged fixtures, uneven flooring, scratched surfaces, or incomplete fittings.
  • ✅ Poor workmanship – Problems caused by substandard building work, including badly installed features, visible defects, or poor-quality finishing.
  • ✅ Unfinished works – Missing items, incomplete installations, or promised works that were not properly completed before or after handover.
  • ✅ Structural or hidden defects – More serious issues such as cracks, leaks, damp, drainage problems, roofing faults, or faulty plumbing and electrics.
  • ✅ Developer delays or lack of response – Cases where repeated complaints are ignored, delayed, or only partly addressed.
  • ✅ Warranty or handover issues – Problems involving what should have been resolved under the new build handover or warranty process.

Your Rights with a New Build Property

Across the UK, buyers of newly built homes are entitled to expect a property that is completed to a reasonable standard, with defects addressed properly and key information provided clearly during the purchase and handover process.

  • Receive a property completed to a reasonable standard – A newly built home should not be handed over with serious defects, incomplete works, or avoidable quality issues.
  • Expect problems to be addressed properly – If defects or snagging issues are identified, developers and builders should respond fairly and take reasonable steps to put them right.
  • Challenge unresolved or unfair outcomes – If defects remain unresolved, repairs are delayed, or the property was not delivered as expected, you may have options available.

In the UK, these protections may be supported by consumer rights, contractual obligations, and relevant property standards, including:

  • Consumer Rights Act 2015 – Helps ensure services connected to the property purchase and delivery are carried out with reasonable care and skill.
  • Misrepresentation Act 1967 – If the property, finish, condition, or promised works were presented inaccurately, this may strengthen your position.
  • New build warranty and handover obligations – Depending on the provider and circumstances, certain defects or issues may fall within the warranty or post-completion process.
